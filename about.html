Hi, I’m Faiz (pronounced fez). I’m a Computing major in the College of Creative Studies at UC Santa Barbara. At the moment, I’m based in the Netherlands, where I’m studying abroad for the next several months.

In the field of computer science, my current interests include network analysis, scalable systems, and functional programming. Currently, I’m focused on Lexcaliber, a suite of network- and NLP-based recommendation and analyis algorithms to aid legal research. My other projects include DrDocx, a computer science education research tool, an automated tabulation system for mock trial tournaments, and various data science projects among other things.

Outside of computer science, I’m passionate about effective altruism, public policy, and constitutional law. Since 2020, I’ve been a member of and volunteer for Giving What We Can, for which I work on various writing and data science projects. I’m also passionate about public policy and constitutional law. My recent writing in these areas includes articles on religious liberty, copyright, and affirmative action law. I also co-founded and currently lead UCSB’s undergraduate moot court team, which has won back-to-back national championships in the American Moot Court Association’s appellate brief competition.

This website is created with Pollen, an open-source publishing system built in Racket. As much as I’m enjoying Racket, I can’t take credit for the incredible work done by Joel Dueck, whose try-pollen project provides much of the code powering this website.
