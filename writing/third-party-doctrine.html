Overturn the Third-Party Doctrine

Established in the mid-1970’s, the third-party doctrine has long given law enforcement free reign to invade people’s privacy without a warrant. In this article, I argue that the doctrine ought to be overturned and examine how the Court can reorient its Fourth Amendment jurisprudence to align with modern expectations of privacy.
